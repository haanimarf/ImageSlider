Australiya
Australia is a country and continent in the Southern Hemisphere, surrounded by the Indian and Pacific Oceans. Its capital is Canberra, with major cities like Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, and Perth. Known for its diverse landscapes, including the Outback, Uluru, and the Great Barrier Reef, Australia has a mostly hot and dry climate, with tropical north and temperate south. English is the main language, and Aboriginal Australians are the original inhabitants. The country has a federal parliamentary democracy, a strong economy based on mining, agriculture, tourism, and education, and unique wildlife such as kangaroos, koalas, and platypuses.
Newzeeland
New Zealand is an island country in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, southeast of Australia, with Wellington as its capital and Auckland as its largest city. It mainly comprises the North and South Islands and is known for stunning landscapes like mountains, glaciers, forests, and lakes. The climate is mild and temperate. English and Māori are spoken, and the Indigenous Māori have a rich cultural heritage. New Zealand has a parliamentary democracy, a strong economy based on agriculture, tourism, and trade, and is famous for dairy, wool, and meat. It is also known for unique wildlife and adventure tourism.
Iceland
Iceland is a small island country in the North Atlantic Ocean, with Reykjavík as its capital and largest city. Known for volcanoes, geysers, glaciers, waterfalls, and hot springs, it has a cold temperate climate with cool summers and mild winters. Icelandic is the main language, and the country has a democratic government with a President and Prime Minister. Its economy relies on fishing, tourism, and renewable energy, and it is famous for its clean environment, safety, and stunning natural landscapes.
Malasiya
Malaysia is a Southeast Asian country consisting of Peninsular Malaysia and East Malaysia on Borneo, with Kuala Lumpur as its capital and Putrajaya as the administrative center. It has a tropical climate with hot, humid weather and heavy rainfall. Malaysia is multicultural, with Malays, Chinese, Indians, and others, and Malay is the main language. The country is a constitutional monarchy with a Prime Minister, and its economy relies on manufacturing, palm oil, tourism, and trade. Malaysia is known for its modern cities, rich natural resources, rainforests, beaches, and cultural heritage.
Ireland
Ireland is an island country in northwestern Europe, with Dublin as its capital and largest city. Known as the “Emerald Isle” for its green landscapes, hills, rivers, and coastlines, it has a mild, temperate climate. English and Irish (Gaelic) are spoken. Ireland is a democratic country with a parliamentary system and a President. Its modern economy focuses on technology, pharmaceuticals, agriculture, and tourism, and it is famous for its rich culture, music, literature, and traditions.
